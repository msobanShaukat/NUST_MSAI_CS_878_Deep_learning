## üìñ Course Description

This graduate-level course provides a comprehensive exploration of the principles, algorithms, and applications of deep neural networks. Covering foundational topics such as feedforward networks, convolutional and recurrent architectures, and extending to advanced subjects like attention mechanisms, Transformers, Vision Transformers, Diffusion Models, and generative models, the course equips students with a deep understanding of both theory and practice. [ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws](https://ppl-ai-file-upload.s3.amazonaws.com/web/direct-files/attachments/77906941/acc0c606-93c6-4bda-b795-30e523989c0a/Outline_CS-878-Deep-Learning.pdf)

Through hands-on assignments and real-world applications in computer vision, natural language processing, and more, students will develop the skills necessary to design, implement, and deploy cutting-edge deep learning models. The course emphasizes interpretability, ethical considerations, and scalability, preparing students for research and industry roles in this rapidly evolving field. ## üéì Course Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to:

- Design, implement, and optimize various deep learning architectures, including convolutional, recurrent, and transformer networks, to solve complex problems in computer vision and natural language processing  
- Gain proficiency in applying advanced deep learning techniques, such as generative models, attention mechanisms, and transfer learning, to develop innovative solutions for real-world challenges  
- Develop a critical understanding of the ethical implications, interpretability, and fairness considerations in the deployment and use of deep learning models, ensuring responsible AI practices
